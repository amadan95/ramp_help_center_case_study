Ramp Help Center Redesign

Personalised knowledge for humans & AI

A metadata-driven, Zendesk-powered prototype that aligns Ramp’s help center with persona-aware delivery, safe AI retrieval, and operator visibility.

Why change? Dense, one-size-fits-all articles slow customers and degrade AI accuracy.
What we built React Native (web) app ingesting live support.ramp.com content, auto-tagging persona, tier, integration, and freshness.
Outcomes Guided experiences for authenticated & anonymous users, RAG-ready chunks, and Ops feedback loops.

The assignment → solution map

  • Authenticated & unauthenticated humans: Filters simulate identity; in production we’d hydrate persona/tier from Ramp auth to auto-tailor the landing.
  • AI RAG consumers: Chunk table exposes safety flags, audiences, and confidence scores for retrieval.
  • Content organization: Articles normalized into knowledge nodes with persona, tier, integration, journey stage, freshness, and feedback metrics.
  • Ops workflow: High impact + stale queues highlight what to fix first, fed by Zendesk votes and update cadence.
  • Feedback loop: Sentiment, recency, and (future) ticket/search telemetry rolled into prioritization.

Live data pipeline

Zendesk + CSV ingest Anonymous GET requests pull categories/sections/articles, and the 30-day usage CSV (447 rows) feeds views & votes for every article.
Metadata inference Keyword heuristics map persona, tier (Base / Plus), integration (QuickBooks, Okta, etc.), journey stage, and regions.
Signals `vote_sum`, `vote_count`, `updated_at` drive sentiment, confidence, and freshness scoring.
Chunk factory Each article renders into RAG chunk with summary, persona constraints, safety flag (`allowed_for_ai`).

Metadata schema (sample)

Core fields

Article id, title, source URL, snippet, freshness (`last_reviewed`), owner team, product variant, region.

Audience overlays

`persona`: employee / admin / bookkeeper / vendor / IT
`service_tier`: Base / Plus
`integrations`: (QuickBooks, Okta, TravelPerk, etc.)

Experience drivers

`content_type`, `journey_stage`, topic cluster, chunk safety flags, confidence scores.

Signals & loops

Votes, calculated sentiment, freshness days, queue urgency. Hooks for future ticket/search telemetry.

Human experience

  • Hero metrics summarise corpus health (articles loaded, votes, positivity).
  • Persona/tier/integration chips act as intent selection for unauthenticated users; authenticated sessions would pre-fill.
  • Article cards show relevance badges, Ramp Plus gating, sentiment pills, and last-review freshness.
  • Clicks deep-link to Zendesk, preserving single source of truth.

Inside the app

Hero & metrics

Live Zendesk sync plus key stats (articles, sentiment, AI chunks, helpful/unhelpful vote mix) keep health indicators visible without clutter.

Audience controls

Persona, tier, and integration filters simulate identity-aware delivery and give anonymous visitors an intent selector.

View switcher

Tabs flip between Human, AI, and Ops modes so each stakeholder sees tailored information without leaving the app.

Human view

Ranks articles by sentiment + freshness, surfaces Ramp Plus gating, and offers one-click access to Zendesk sources. Usage placeholders appear when only CSV data exists.

AI view

Presents chunk metadata: persona/tier, summary, confidence, and safety flag—exact inputs for a RAG service.

Operator console

Queues highlight high-impact refreshes and stale content, with AI-generated suggestions describing the gap to address.

Core tenets in the UI

Accuracy Sentiment metric (positive-vote rate) and chunk confidence gating surface risky articles for review; AI view hides unsafe content.
Efficiency Persona/tier/integration filters collapse navigation paths so authenticated and anonymous users land on relevant guidance fast.
Coverage Chunk count and topic coverage keep zero-result scenarios rare; fallback messaging prompts broader filters.
User & operator experience Operator console pairs queue priority with AI suggestions, while the UI stays light for customers.

AI retrieval view

  • Chunk table filters to the same persona/tier controls used in the human view.
  • Confidence blends freshness + sentiment to safeguard accuracy; low scores feed the Ops queue.
  • `allowed_for_ai` prevents Plus-only or sensitive content from leaking into public bots.
  • Each chunk links back to the canonical article for citation and human audit.

Operator console

  • High impact refresh queue: High-traffic or low-sentiment articles bubble to the top with persona/tier context.
  • Stale content list: Auto-detects articles untouched for 90+ days to protect accuracy.
  • Cards link to Zendesk for editing; README outlines structured authoring, review workflow, and embedding refresh.

Feedback & improvement loop

Live telemetry Votes, last updated, and persona filters already feed prioritisation.
Planned signals Zendesk ticket deflection, search queries with zero results, in-product retry metrics.
AI safety Confidence thresholds route shaky responses to manual review; version history resolves regressions.
Success metrics Reduce support downvotes 50%, cut solved-session depth 25%, speed median article refresh to <5 days.

Roadmap & next steps

  • Phase 1: Productionise schema in CMS, backfill top 100 articles, add authenticated pre-filters.
  • Phase 2: Integrate telemetry dashboards, structured authoring UI, automated linting & embedding refresh.
  • Phase 3: Deploy hybrid vector/keyword retrieval, connect to live support bots, close ticket-loop automation.
  • Ask: Align on data contracts with Zendesk + auth, prioritise instrumentation, and pilot with operations team.
